1 Question 1

{mdframed}

The three pauli matricies are given by:

X^=[0110],Y^=[0−ii0],Z^=[100−1]

Find the eigenvectors, eigenvalues, and diagonal representations of X^, Y^, and Z^.

First find eigenvalues by solving the characteristic equation.

1.1 For X^

Characteristic polynomial: λ2−1. Therefore, the eigenvalues are λ=±1.

In a diagnolized matrix, the eigenvaluesa are the entries along the diagonal. Therefore the diagnolized form of X^ is

[100−1]

Finally, to find the eigenvectors, we solve the equation (X^−λ⁢I)⁢v→=0 for each eigenvalue. For λ=1, we have.

[−111−1]⁢[xy] =0
⟹−x+y=0
x−y=0
∴v→ =[11]

For λ=−1, we have

[1111]⁢[xy] =0
⟹x+y =0
x+y =0
∴v→ =[−11]

1.2 For Y^

Characteristic polynomial: λ2−1. Therefore, the eigenvalues are λ=±1.

Hence, the diagnolized matrix is the same as that for X^.

[100−1]

Finally, to find the eigenvectors, we solve the equation (Y^−λ⁢I)⁢v→=0 for each eigenvalue. For λ=1, we have.

[−1−ii−1]⁢[xy] =0
⟹−x−i⁢y=0
i⁢x−y=0
∴v→ =[i1]

For λ=−1, we have

[1−ii1]⁢[xy] =0
⟹x−i⁢y =0
i⁢x+y =0
∴v→ =[−i1]

1.3 For Z^

Notice that Z^ is already diagonal. The eigenvalues are the entries along the diagonal, which are λ=±1.

The eigenvectors are trivially v→=[10] and v→=[01] for λ=1 and λ=−1 respectively.

2 Question 2

{mdframed}

Show that

X^⁢Y^=i⁢Z^,Y^⁢Z^=i⁢X^,Z^⁢X^=i⁢Y^

and hence X^⁢Z^⁢X^=−Z^

2.1 Part (a)

X^⁢Y^ =[0110]⋅[0−ii0]
=[i00−i]
=i⋅[100−1]
=i⁢Z^
Y^⁢Z^ =[0−ii0]⋅[100−1]
=[0ii0]
=i⋅[0101]
=i⁢X^
X^⁢Y^ =[0110]⋅[100−1]
=[0−110]
=i⋅[0−ii0]
=i⁢Y^

2.2 Part (b)

Substiute Z^⁢X^=i⁢Y^.

X^⁢Z^⁢X^ =X^⁢(i⁢Y^)
=i⁢X^⁢Y^
Substiute⁢X^⁢Y^=i⁢Z^
=i⁢(i⁢Z^)
=−Z^

3 Question 3

{mdframed}

A unitary matrix is a matrix whose inverse is equal to its Hermitian adjoint. (i.e take the transpose and the complex conjugate). Show that

U^=[ab−b∗a∗]

Where (|a|2+|b|2=1) is unitary.

Find the Hermitian adjoint of U^. I.e we find U^†=(U^T)∗

U^† =(U^T)∗
=([ab−b∗a∗]∗)T=[a−b∗ba∗]∗
=[a∗−bb∗a]

Now, for a matrix to be unitary, it must mean that U^⁢U^†=I. We can check this.

[ab−b∗a∗]⋅[a∗−bb∗a] =[a⁢a∗+b⁢b∗−a⁢b−b⁢a−b∗⁢a∗+a∗⁢b∗b∗⁢b+a∗⁢a]
=[|a|2+|b|200|a|2+|b|2]
=[1001]

Therefore, U^ is unitary.
