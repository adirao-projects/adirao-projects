Question 1

The three pauli matricies are given by:

$$\hat{X} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}, \ \hat{Y} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0\end{bmatrix}, \ \hat{Z} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}$$

Find the eigenvectors, eigenvalues, and diagonal representations of X̂, Ŷ, and Ẑ.

First find eigenvalues by solving the characteristic equation.

For X̂

Characteristic polynomial: λ2 − 1. Therefore, the eigenvalues are λ = ±1.

In a diagnolized matrix, the eigenvaluesa are the entries along the diagonal. Therefore the diagnolized form of X̂ is

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}$$

Finally, to find the eigenvectors, we solve the equation (X̂ − λI)v⃗ = 0 for each eigenvalue. For λ = 1, we have.

$$\begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} &= 0 \\ \implies - x + y = 0 \\ x - y = 0 \\ \therefore \vec{v} &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

For λ = −1, we have

$$\begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} &= 0 \\ \implies x + y &= 0 \\ x + y &= 0 \\ \therefore \vec{v} &= \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

For Ŷ

Characteristic polynomial: λ2 − 1. Therefore, the eigenvalues are λ = ±1.

Hence, the diagnolized matrix is the same as that for X̂.

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}$$

Finally, to find the eigenvectors, we solve the equation (Ŷ − λI)v⃗ = 0 for each eigenvalue. For λ = 1, we have.

$$\begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & -i \\ i & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} &= 0 \\ \implies - x - iy = 0 \\ ix - y = 0 \\ \therefore \vec{v} &= \begin{bmatrix} i \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

For λ = −1, we have

$$\begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -i \\ i & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} &= 0 \\ \implies x - iy &= 0 \\ ix + y &= 0 \\ \therefore \vec{v} &= \begin{bmatrix} -i \\ 1\end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

For Ẑ

Notice that Ẑ is already diagonal. The eigenvalues are the entries along the diagonal, which are λ = ±1.

The eigenvectors are trivially $\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0\end{bmatrix}$ and $\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1\end{bmatrix}$ for λ = 1 and λ = −1 respectively.

Question 2

Show that X̂Ŷ = iẐ, ŶẐ = iX̂, ẐX̂ = iŶ

and hence X̂ẐX̂ = −Ẑ

Part (a)

$$\begin{aligned} \hat{X}\hat{Y} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}\cdot \begin{bmatrix}0 & -i \\ i & 0\end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} i & 0 \\ 0 & -i\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\hat{Z} \end{aligned}$$

$$\begin{aligned} \hat{Y}\hat{Z} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0\end{bmatrix}\cdot \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & i \\ i & 0\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\hat{X} \end{aligned}$$

$$\begin{aligned} \hat{X}\hat{Y} &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}\cdot \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0\end{bmatrix} \\ &= i\hat{Y} \end{aligned}$$

Part (b)

Substiute ẐX̂ = iŶ. $$\begin{aligned} \hat{X}\hat{Z}\hat{X} &= \hat{X}(i\hat{Y}) \\ &= i\hat{X}\hat{Y} \\ &\text{Substiute} \ \hat{X}\hat{Y} = i\hat{Z} \\ &= i(i\hat{Z}) \\ &= -\hat{Z} \end{aligned}$$

Question 3

A unitary matrix is a matrix whose inverse is equal to its Hermitian adjoint. (i.e take the transpose and the complex conjugate). Show that $$\hat{U} = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ -b^* & a^* \end{bmatrix}$$

Where (|a|2 + |b|2 = 1) is unitary.

Find the Hermitian adjoint of Û. I.e we find Û† = (ÛT)*

$$\begin{aligned} \hat{U}^{\dagger} &= (\hat{U}^T)^* \\ &= \left(\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ -b^* & a^* \end{bmatrix}^*\right)^T = \begin{bmatrix} a & -b^* \\ b & a^* \end{bmatrix}^* \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} a^* & -b \\ b^* & a \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

Now, for a matrix to be unitary, it must mean that ÛÛ† = I. We can check this.

$$\begin{aligned} \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ -b^* & a^* \end{bmatrix}\cdot\begin{bmatrix} a^* & -b \\ b^* & a \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} aa^* + bb^* & -ab - ba \\ -b^*a^* + a^*b^* & b^*b + a^*a \end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} |a|^2 + |b|^2 & 0 \\ 0 & |a|^2 + |b|^2 \end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned}$$

Therefore, Û is unitary.
